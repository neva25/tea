Finest Darjeeling Tea

Glenburn Tea Estate is one of the finest in Darjeeling, where the magnificent peaks of the 3rd highest mountain in the world, Mount Kanchenjunga, ascend over the tea bushes. It's name literally means: A River Valley. Here in the hills of Darjeeling, a flourishing community of 700 workers and tea bushes thrive as they have done together for the past 150 years, since the time of their ancestors. Nestled in 350 hectares of reserve forest that sprawls the 750 hectare estate, and with two snow-fed Himalayan rivers nourishing Glenburn tea in their path, Glenburn is in a unique geographical location that brings you a collection of teas that cannot be replicated anywhere else in the world. Started by a Scottish Tea Company in 1859, Glenburn now belongs to one of India's pioneering tea planting families-The Prakashes. Legend has it that the spirit of Glenburn's first manager, Kimble Murray, is still walking these slopes,his soul unable to rest until the quest for a perfect cup of tea is complete.This sentiment is very much in-keeping with the Glenburn philosophy today: "It's not just the rain and the sun... there's a soul there we have to look after and that's what gives our Glenburn tea, that very special flavour"
